SECTION 1 — OVERVIEW
==============================================================

TOPIC: What is the Frontier Partner specialization?
The Frontier Partner specialization is the premier recognition for
Scale Solution Providers, resellers, and systems integrators (SIs)
that can design, build, deploy, govern, and secure AI agents across
the full Microsoft Frontier Transformation stack. That stack includes
Microsoft 365 Copilot, Microsoft GitHub, Microsoft Azure AI Foundry,
Microsoft Fabric, and key security products.
It was announced at the State of the Partner Ecosystem 2026.
It is the next evolution of partner recognition for the AI agent era.
It replaces the previous Frontier Partner badge with a more rigorous,
audited credential. The credential gives customers and Microsoft field
teams a verified signal of a partner organization's end-to-end
capabilities.
Partners who earn it gain stronger differentiation with customers,
deeper visibility with Microsoft field teams, and access to a richer
set of Microsoft AI Cloud Partner Program benefits than the badge
provided.

TOPIC: How is the specialization different from the current Frontier
Partner badge?
The specialization raises the bar from the badge across three
dimensions: which specializations are required, the skilling
investment expected from the partner's team, and the addition of an
annual agentic audit.

Side-by-side comparison of requirements:

- REQUIRED SPECIALIZATIONS
  Frontier Partner badge (current): Microsoft Copilot, Data Security,
  and one of three Azure AI specializations — AI Apps on Microsoft
  Azure, AI Platform on Microsoft Azure, or Agentic DevOps with
  Microsoft Azure and GitHub.
  Frontier Partner specialization (future): Microsoft Copilot; AI Apps
  on Microsoft Azure OR AI Platform on Microsoft Azure; Data Security;
  and Identity & Access Management.

- SKILLING
  Frontier Partner badge (current): None.
  Frontier Partner specialization (future): Five individuals complete
  the Frontier Transformation Engineer learning path; three
  individuals earn the Fabric Analytics Engineer Associate
  certification (DP-600).

- AUDIT
  Frontier Partner badge (current): None.
  Frontier Partner specialization (future): An annual audit validating
  capability to design, build, deploy, govern, and secure AI agents
  across the Frontier stack.

- EVALUATION CADENCE
  Frontier Partner badge (current): Rolling 10-day window.
  Frontier Partner specialization (future): Annual assessment.
  The change to an annual assessment gives partners a more stable and
  predictable path to maintain the credential.

TOPIC: Who is the specialization designed for?
It is designed for Scale Solution Providers, systems integrators, and
resellers that serve enterprise, small and medium-sized business
(SMB), and small, medium, and corporate (SMC) customers across all
industries. Specifically, it recognizes partners that work directly
with customers to design, build, deploy, and manage AI agents as part
of their delivery practice.
If a partner's business model primarily focuses on enabling other
resellers or managing channel distribution rather than direct customer
delivery, the Frontier Distributor designation is the more relevant
offering.

TOPIC: Why is Microsoft introducing this now?
AI has moved from pilots to production. Customers are deploying agents,
embedding them in business processes, and expecting governance,
security, and measurable outcomes from day one.
Microsoft Frontier Transformation is the framework for how partners
design, build, deploy, govern, and secure AI agents across Azure,
M365, GitHub, and key security products. The specialization recognizes
partners who can deliver across that entire platform, not just
individual products.
It expands on the badge to give customers and Microsoft sellers high
confidence when choosing a provider: partners who hold it have been
vetted, not just enrolled.

SECTION 2 — REQUIREMENTS
==============================================================

TOPIC: What are the specific requirements to earn the specialization?
Partners must meet all three of the following components
simultaneously, all associated with the same Partner Global Account.

COMPONENT 1 — Four specializations (held at the same time on the same
Partner Global Account):
  - Microsoft Copilot (with audit)
  - AI Apps on Microsoft Azure OR AI Platform on Microsoft Azure
  - Data Security
  - Identity & Access Management

COMPONENT 2 — Skilling:
  - Five individuals complete the forthcoming Frontier Transformation
    Engineer learning path and receive the associated badge.
  - Three individuals earn the Fabric Analytics Engineer Associate
    certification (DP-600).
  - The individuals earning the Frontier Transformation Engineer
    badge and those earning DP-600 can be the same or different
    people, but all must be associated with the same Partner Global
    Account.

COMPONENT 3 — Agentic audit:
  - An annual audit validating the organization's ability to design,
    build, deploy, govern, and secure AI agents across Copilot,
    Copilot Studio, GitHub, Fabric, Foundry, and agent security
    controls.
  - NOTE: The exact audit criteria are still being finalized.
    Microsoft's team will contact partners by email when more details
    are available.

TOPIC: What is the Frontier Transformation Engineer learning path?
To earn the specialization, five individuals must complete the
forthcoming Frontier Transformation Engineer learning path. It is a
training course designed for solutions engineers and solutions
architects. Those who complete the course receive a badge signifying
their skills. The learning path comprises 80+ hours of learning
organized into three stages:

  STAGE 1 — Certifications (available now): Three certifications
  establish the technical foundation:
    - GH-300 GitHub Copilot Associate
    - AI-102 / AI-103 Azure AI Engineer Associate
    - AB-100 Agentic AI Business Solutions Architect

  STAGE 2 — Project-ready workshops (available June 2026): Hands-on
  delivery training across:
    - M365 Copilot + Copilot Studio
    - GitHub Copilot + Azure AI Foundry
    - Data Infrastructure + Analytics (Fabric)
    - AI Governance

  STAGE 3 — Frontier COE & Hypervelocity Engineering (available June
  2026): Advanced content covering AI Center of Excellence creation,
  the Hypervelocity Engineering Framework, and industry-specific agent
  development.

The associated badge becomes available to an individual once all three
stages are complete. Detailed learning path information and enrollment
options are available through the Partner Skilling Hub. The Frontier
Engineer Launch Summit is scheduled for partners on June 9, with
registration at aka.ms/FrontierPartnerSummit.

TOPIC: Do all five Frontier Transformation Engineers need to earn the
same certifications?
Yes. Each individual completing the Frontier Transformation Engineer
learning path follows the same path, including all required
certifications and project-ready workshops. The five individuals can
be drawn from across the organization and do not need to be in the
same role, team, or geography, as long as all are associated with the
same Partner Global Account.

TOPIC: What does the agentic audit evaluate?
The audit validates the organization's real-world capability to
deliver agents across the Frontier stack. While certifications show
that the organization has invested in the necessary skilling, the
audit demonstrates that the team can design, build, deploy, govern,
and secure agents in customer environments using Copilot, Copilot
Studio, GitHub Copilot, Azure AI Foundry, Microsoft Fabric, and agent
security controls including Defender, Entra, and Purview.
Additional details on audit criteria and the assessment process will
be made available ahead of the July 2026 general availability.
Partners with questions in the meantime can ask their Microsoft
contact to connect them to the right resources.

SECTION 3 — BENEFITS
==============================================================

TOPIC: What benefits do partners receive when they earn the
specialization?
The specialization comes with a richer benefit bundle than the current
badge. The benefits span four key areas:

  - CUSTOMER & SELLER DIFFERENTIATION: A customer-facing
    specialization badge that can be used in marketing materials, plus
    visibility with Microsoft field sellers — so the organization is
    surfaced when customers search for validated agentic AI delivery
    capability.

  - CO-SELL: Expanded co-sell eligibility, creating direct revenue
    upside for partners who have invested in full-stack Frontier
    delivery.

  - PRODUCT BENEFITS: Access to Microsoft Agent Pre-Purchase Plan (P3)
    credits and Microsoft 365 E7 product licensing for internal use,
    enabling the team to build, test, and demonstrate agents on the
    Microsoft platform.

  - MARKETING RESOURCES: Go-to-market (GTM) resources, including
    Partner of the Year Award eligibility, sponsored AI coaching, case
    study opportunities, and an executive press release template.

Additional GTM benefit details will be shared closer to the July 2026
general availability.

TOPIC: Do partners keep their Frontier Partner badge benefits while
working toward the specialization?
Yes. If a partner is currently enrolled in the Frontier Partner badge,
existing badge benefits remain fully in place during the transition.
The specialization is the path forward, and Microsoft is committed to
supporting badge holders through their progression. Badge benefits
remain intact until the next anniversary date of the badge, so the
partner has access to them while working toward the new requirements.

SECTION 4 — TRANSITION FOR CURRENT FRONTIER PARTNER BADGE HOLDERS
==============================================================

TOPIC: A partner currently holds the Frontier Partner badge — what
does this change mean for them?
The Frontier Partner badge will be retired at the end of FY27. As a
current badge holder, the partner will have access to resources to
help attain the specialization:
  - The partner is part of a select group that Microsoft wants to
    transition to the specialization as a priority. The partner's
    Microsoft contact will reach out directly to support the path. If
    the partner has no assigned Microsoft contact, a member of the
    Microsoft AI Cloud Partner Program Concierge team will reach out.
  - The partner keeps the badge as long as they continue to meet the
    badge requirements in FY27, or until they enroll in the
    specialization, whichever is sooner.
  - Current badge benefits remain in place during the transition.

WHITE-GLOVE SUPPORT FOR BADGE HOLDERS: The Microsoft AI Cloud Partner
Program Concierge team will reach out directly to support the partner
on the Frontier Transformation Engineer learning path and the DP-600
skilling requirements. If the partner needs to close any remaining
specialization prerequisites, the Concierge team will also be in
contact to help build a plan.

TOPIC: Why is Microsoft raising the bar now?
Microsoft Frontier Transformation is the framework for how partners
design, build, deploy, govern, and secure AI agents across Azure,
M365, GitHub, and key security products. The specialization expands on
the badge to signal to customers and sellers that a partner can
deliver across the entire stack, not just parts of it. The
requirements reflect the capabilities required to deliver Frontier
Transformation end-to-end at production scale.

TOPIC: Will a partner's badge automatically convert to the
specialization?
No. The specialization requires meeting the new criteria, including
the four prerequisite specializations, the skilling requirements, and
passing the audit. Holding the badge does not automatically grant the
specialization. However, badge holders receive meaningful transition
support, including the ability to maintain the badge during FY27 as
long as requirements are met, and direct outreach from Microsoft
Partner Concierge teams to help plan and close any remaining gaps.

SECTION 5 — TIMELINE & ELIGIBILITY
==============================================================

TOPIC: When can partners enroll?
The Frontier Partner specialization will be announced at MCAPS Start
for Partners on July 22, 2026, with enrollment opening at the same
time. Partners who currently hold the Frontier Partner badge are
invited to opt in to a private preview ahead of general availability;
Microsoft will reach out directly via email to those organizations.
Certification training for the Frontier Transformation Engineer
learning path is available now. Project-ready workshops begin in June
2026.

TOPIC: How does a partner check where their organization stands
against the requirements today?
Partner Center admins can check the status of the prerequisite
specializations directly in the Partner Center Membership Workspace
today. The specializations overview page displays which of the four
required specializations the organization currently holds and which
remain outstanding.
Partners with questions about their current status can ask their
Microsoft contacts for help assessing their position.

TOPIC: A partner meets most requirements but has one or two gaps — is
support available?
Yes. The Partner Concierge team actively works with partners who are
close to qualifying to help identify gaps and build a plan to close
them. If a partner has been contacted by Microsoft about the
specialization, this support is already part of the transition
experience. If a partner has not been contacted and believes they are
close to qualifying, they should reach out to their Partner
Development Manager (PDM) or Partner Technology Strategist (PTS) to get
connected with Partner Concierge. Concierge engagements begin in June.

SECTION 6 — MICROSOFT AI CLOUD PARTNER PROGRAM LANDSCAPE
==============================================================

TOPIC: How does the Frontier Partner specialization fit into the
broader Microsoft AI Cloud Partner Program?
The Frontier Partner specialization is one of several program
offerings Microsoft is introducing as part of the Frontier
Transformation framework. There are two key offerings that validate
Frontier Transformation capabilities:
  - For channel/SI partners (Scale Solution Providers, indirect
    resellers, systems integrators): the Frontier Partner
    specialization recognizes hands-on delivery capability for
    customers.
  - For Cloud Solution Provider (CSP) distributors: the Frontier
    Distributor designation recognizes channel enablement capability.

TOPIC: How is the Frontier Partner specialization different from the
Frontier Distributor designation?
They target different partner business models:
  - The Frontier Partner specialization is for partners that work
    directly with customers to design, build, deploy, and manage AI
    agents. It recognizes hands-on technical delivery capability
    across the full Microsoft Frontier stack.
  - The Frontier Distributor designation is for CSP distributors whose
    role is to enable their indirect reseller channel to build,
    deploy, and manage agents. It recognizes channel enablement and
    capability, not direct customer delivery.
In short: the specialization is about delivering agents to customers;
the distributor designation is about enabling partners and growing
agent sales through the channel. Partners should pursue the offering
that best suits their business model. In some cases, both may be
pursued.

SECTION 7 — GETTING STARTED
==============================================================

TOPIC: What should a partner organization do right now?
The most important near-term action is to start the skilling track —
specifically, enroll individuals in the Frontier Transformation
Engineer learning path. Certification training is available today and
does not require waiting for enrollment to open in July 2026.
Beyond skilling, the recommended actions are:
  - Register for the Frontier Engineer Launch Summit on June 9 at
    aka.ms/FrontierPartnerSummit.
  - Check specialization status: in the Partner Center Membership
    Workspace, the Partner Center admin can view which of the four
    prerequisite specializations the organization holds and identify
    any gaps.
  - Identify five individuals for the Frontier Transformation Engineer
    learning path and three individuals for DP-600. They can be the
    same or different people. Starting now can reduce the total time
    to specialization eligibility.
  - Engage the PDM or PTS with questions about readiness or for
    support in building a plan.

TOPIC: Where can a partner learn more?
Resources to learn more about the Frontier Partner specialization:
  - Partner Center Membership Workspace: check current specialization
    status.
  - Partner Skilling Hub: enroll in the Frontier Transformation
    Engineer learning path and access role-based resources.
  - State of the Partner Ecosystem 2026 blog: Nicole Dezen's overview
    of Frontier Transformation and partner program direction.
  - The partner's Partner Development Manager or Partner Technology
    Strategist: continue the readiness conversation and make a plan to
    close requirement gaps.
If a partner was contacted by Microsoft about the specialization,
their Microsoft contact is the primary point of support for questions
not answered here.
